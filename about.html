My Story

From traumatic brain injury to helping others transform their bodies. This is why I coach, why I study peptides, and why I believe everyone deserves a science-based approach to getting stronger.

William Marceau — Naples, FL

The Backstory

A traumatic brain injury changed everything. One moment I was living my life; the next, I was starting over from zero. Physically broken. Mentally foggy. The kind of setback that makes most people question whether they'll ever feel normal again.

I didn't just want normal. I wanted stronger.

Recovery wasn't linear and it sure wasn't fast. But it taught me something most people never learn the hard way: your body is an incredibly adaptive machine when you give it the right inputs. The problem is that most fitness advice out there is built on trends, not science. I needed something that actually worked.

So I went deep. I studied evidence-based training methodologies. I learned how the body actually recovers and rebuilds at a hormonal and cellular level. I stopped listening to Instagram coaches and started reading the research. And piece by piece, I rebuilt myself — stronger than I was before the injury.

Why Peptides

My recovery journey led me straight into the world of peptide science. Not the bro science version you see on social media — the actual clinical research. I spent hundreds of hours studying GH secretagogues, Tesamorelin, recovery compounds, and how they interact with training and nutrition at a physiological level.

I wanted to understand why my recovery was working. What was happening at the hormonal level when certain protocols clicked. Why some approaches accelerated healing while others did nothing. The answers kept pointing back to peptide signaling pathways.

Before long, I became the guy friends called when they wanted to understand what peptides could actually do versus the hype. Not because I was selling anything — because I'd done the work to separate clinical evidence from marketing noise. That distinction matters, and it's something most people in this space either can't or won't make.
